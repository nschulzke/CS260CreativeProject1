What is a conlang?

The word conlang stands for Constructed Language. A conlang is a language that is designed and created, as opposed to one that organically develops. These languages can be designed for fun, or to serve a purpose.

Some of the most prominent examples of conlangs are used in fictional worlds. These include Tolkien's Elvish languages of Quenya and Sindarin, as well as Star Trek's Klingon lanugage. Conlangs have recently featured in movies and TV series as well, including languages by David J. Peterson in Thor 2: The Dark World and Game of Thrones.

Constructing a language involves designing its phonology (sounds), lexicon (words), and grammar. Optionally, some language creators (such as Tolkien) also design writing systems for their languages. There's a great introduction to language creation called The Language Construction Kit, by Mark Rosenfelder.

What is LangHub?

Langhub is designed to make sharing your conlangs easy! It will provide features to make working with other languages easy, including spellcheck for your conlang, auto-replacement of digraphs with special characters, and more!
